Heterodox Econ Courses

A collection of computational-economics courses built as Quarto books with executable Python, interactive widgets, and Obsidian notes. Each course has a polished book track and a working notes track.

The Calculation Course
A Computational-Economics Curriculum from Multivariable Calculus to Climate-Constrained Planning
Ten weeks of mathematical foundations (calculus, linear algebra, optimization, input-output analysis, differential equations) followed by seven in-depth heterodox-economics controversies (Cobb-Douglas, socialist calculation, transformation problem, Sraffa, Okishio, monetary theory, climate planning). Designed to get you reading Morishima and Cockshott/Cottrell with full mathematical comprehension.

The Crypto Calculation Course
Cryptocurrency, Blockchain, and the Ecosocialist Planning System
Examines cryptocurrency and blockchain technology through the lens of heterodox political economy. Covers cryptographic foundations, Bitcoin's Austrian ideological roots, Ethereum and programmable money, DeFi financialization, and the ecosocialist critique — asking what a socialist blockchain would actually need to do. Prerequisite: Calculation Course through Week 8 and Controversies 2 and 6.
