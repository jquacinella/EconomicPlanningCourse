# Glossary

Optional shared vocabulary as the project grows. Add terms here when a definition belongs everywhere instead of buried in a single week's notes.

## Terms

<!-- Format: term — short definition. Cross-link with [[wiki-links]] where useful. -->

- **Naturalrechnung** — "calculation in kind." Otto Neurath's term for economic planning conducted in physical units (kWh, tons, hectares, hours) without reduction to a common monetary scalar. Treated in Controversy 2, Week 8, Controversy 8, and Appendix D.
- **Calculation in kind** — English rendering of *Naturalrechnung*; sometimes also "in-kind calculation" or "in-natura planning." Distinct from Cockshott's labor-time-monist sense of "in natura" (which retains a single common scalar — labor hours).
- **Lebenslage** (pl. *Lebenslagen*) — "life-situation." Neurath's term for the multi-dimensional state of a household, region, or society described as a vector of physical magnitudes (food, shelter, work-time, health, environment). A plan is, in Neurath's framework, a choice among feasible *Lebenslagen*.
- **Pareto frontier** (planning sense) — the set of feasible plans for which no other feasible plan dominates on every dimension. Comparison within the frontier requires further political-deliberative choice. Construction techniques: Pareto-dominance partial order, lexicographic ordering, weighted-sum scalarization, ε-constraint method.
- **Scalarization** — the act of reducing a multi-dimensional objective vector to a single scalar via choice of weights. The choice is a smuggled-in exchange rate between non-commensurable dimensions; the Neurath-O'Neill claim is that the choice is irreducibly political and pretending otherwise (e.g., by treating market prices as the politically-neutral scalarization) is the actual error.
- **Incommensurability** (ecological-economics sense) — Martinez-Alier and O'Neill's claim, descended from Neurath, that environmental decisions involve qualitatively heterogeneous values that cannot be reduced to a monetary scalar without distortion.
- **ε-constraint method** — multi-criteria optimization technique: optimize one objective with the others as bounded constraints; sweep the bounds to trace the Pareto frontier. The dominant practical method for multi-objective LP and the most direct rendering of Neurath's procedure in modern operations research.
- **Isotype** — the *International System of Typographic Picture Education*, descended from Neurath's *Wiener Methode der Bildstatistik* (1925). A visual language for communicating economic and social statistics to a non-specialist public; the ancestor of modern infographic and dashboard design. Off the mathematical spine but on the spine of any serious democratic-planning project.
