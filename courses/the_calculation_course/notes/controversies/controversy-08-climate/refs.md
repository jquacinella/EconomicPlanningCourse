# References — Controversy 8 — Climate-Constrained Planning

_Papers, books, side topics to come back to._

## Core text and reviews

- Cockshott, Cottrell & Dapprich, *Economic Planning in an Age of Climate Crisis* (Amazon self-published, 2022, ISBN 9798357728739).
- Michael Roberts, "Planning and the Climate," *The Next Recession* (Dec 2022).
- *Cosmonaut*, "How (Not) to Economically Plan in the Age of Climate Crisis" (July 2023).

## The Neurath–Kapp–Martinez-Alier–O'Neill ecological-economics lineage

- Otto Neurath, *Economic Writings 1904–1945*, ed. Uebel & Cohen (Kluwer 2004), and "Total Socialization" (1920) and "Economic Plan and Calculation in Kind" (1925).
- K. William Kapp, *The Social Costs of Private Enterprise* (Harvard 1950). Bridge from Neurath to modern ecological economics.
- Joan Martinez-Alier, *Ecological Economics: Energy, Environment and Society* (Blackwell 1987), especially Chapter 11.
- Joan Martinez-Alier, Giuseppe Munda & John O'Neill, "Weak Comparability of Values as a Foundation for Ecological Economics" (*Ecological Economics* 26:3, 1998).
- John O'Neill, "Socialist Calculation and Environmental Valuation: Money, Markets and Ecology" (*Science & Society* 66:1, 2002).
- John O'Neill, *Markets, Deliberation and Environment* (Routledge 2007), Chapter 4.
- Clive Spash, various papers on incommensurability.
- Kohei Saito, *Marx in the Anthropocene* (Cambridge 2023).

## Left counter-proposal and mainstream comparison

- Troy Vettese & Drew Pendergrass, *Half-Earth Socialism* (Verso 2022). Interactive simulation at <https://half.earth>.
- Nicholas Stern, *Stern Review* (2006), executive summary.
- William Nordhaus, Nobel lecture.
