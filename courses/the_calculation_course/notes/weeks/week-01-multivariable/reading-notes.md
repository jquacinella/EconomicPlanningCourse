Key ideas from the first lecture:
- Partial derivatives: hold all variables constant except one, differentiate with respect to that one
- Gradient $\nabla f$ points in the direction of steepest ascent
- The gradient is perpendicular to level curves — this will matter a lot for constrained optimization

**Question I have:** Why does the gradient being perpendicular to level curves follow from the definition? I can see it geometrically but not yet algebraically. → See [[questions]]

**Connection to economics:** The gradient of a utility function gives the marginal utilities. The ratio of marginal utilities is the MRS. This is Week 2 territory but already visible.
